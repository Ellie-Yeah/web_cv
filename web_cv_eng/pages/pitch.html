What do they have in common: a restaurant, a hotel, a handful of entrepreneurs and a mental health institution?


Well, seeing it just like that, they don't seem to have anything in common.

But if you look closely, in all of them there is a person who has a problem and needs help to solve it.


Since I've started working, one of the things that gives me the most satisfaction is seeing a person happy after having helped them.


  • It can be as simple as having a fast, efficient and friendly service while attending tables.
  • Observing attentively and going a bit further, exceeding the expectations that people had for their stay.
  • By creating work methods that facilitate and help in the team's dailywork.
  • Or using creativity to create something unique that attracts the attention of potential customers.
  • Managing the online presence of an entrepreneur so that they can focus on their business.
  • Or it can be purely taking care of those who need it most, teaching them patiently useful skills for their future.

Yeah, I'm just like that, costumer-focused, someone who works hard to provide the best service, following quality standards, and always looking foward to continuously improve.
I use my empathy to put myself in the other person's shoes, to better understandtheir problems and thus finding the best solutions to solve them, or acting to prevent problems from arising in the first place.
And when I don't know something... I have no problem researching and studying more, or asking someone more experienced, to know how to overcome a certain obstacle.

I'm looking for a new opportunity where I can use my skills and experience to help others, and where I can continue to learn and grow as a professional.
